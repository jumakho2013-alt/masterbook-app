# How to make MasterBook feel UNDER-priced (research synthesis, ~1800 words)

## 1. Job-to-be-done value framing — 15 jobs and stacked monthly value

Per the JTBD framework, products win when they help customers get their jobs done "better and/or more cheaply" [Strategyn]. A solo provider doesn't pay for "a CRM" — they pay to get specific jobs done. Below are 15 jobs MasterBook is hired for, with conservative monthly RUB value-per-job for a typical solo master earning ~80 000 ₽/mo (effective hourly ≈ 500 ₽).

| # | Job | Alt cost | Monthly value |
|---|-----|----------|---------------|
| 1 | Remember who the client is and what they like | 30 min/wk searching chats → 2 h × 500 | **1 000 ₽** |
| 2 | Avoid no-shows via reminder 1 h before | Industry avg no-show 23 %; SMS reminders cut 38–50 % [AgentZap; MessageMedia]. 1 saved slot/wk × 1 500 ₽ avg ticket × 4 | **6 000 ₽** |
| 3 | Re-book lapsed clients before they drift to a competitor | 2 reactivated clients/mo × 1 500 ₽ | **3 000 ₽** |
| 4 | Track income vs expense without Excel | 3 h/mo × 500 | **1 500 ₽** |
| 5 | Produce tax-ready summary for self-employed tax (НПД) | 1 fine avoided/year ÷ 12 (5 000 ₽/yr) | **400 ₽** |
| 6 | Photo records of work for portfolio + dispute defense | 1 dispute avoided/yr ÷ 12 (≈ 5 000 ₽) | **400 ₽** |
| 7 | Schedule visualization to prevent double-bookings | 1 conflict/mo × 1 500 ₽ | **1 500 ₽** |
| 8 | Service price list always accessible (no "how much was that?") | 15 min/wk × 4 × 500/60 | **500 ₽** |
| 9 | Work-hours/break planning → energy preservation | Intangible burnout avoidance | **500 ₽** |
| 10 | Confidence projecting "professional" to clients | Premium pricing tolerance +5 % on 80 000 ₽ | **4 000 ₽** |
| 11 | Backup of client base if phone is lost/stolen | 1 incident/3 yrs of full client loss ≈ 30 000 ₽ ÷ 36 | **800 ₽** |
| 12 | Quick onboarding for a new helper/apprentice | 1× per year × 2 h × 500 ÷ 12 | **100 ₽** |
| 13 | Filter clients by service/visit recency for promos | 1 promo/mo, 3 takers × 1 500 ₽ | **4 500 ₽** |
| 14 | Privacy of client data (no Google Sheets leak) | Reputational risk floor | **500 ₽** |
| 15 | "Mental peace" — one place for everything | Cognitive load reduction | **1 000 ₽** |

**Stacked monthly value ≈ 25 700 ₽** vs **299 ₽** sticker → ~86× value/price ratio. Even discounting heavily for over-counting, 10× ratio remains. Field-service vendors already advertise this framing: "platforms claim they pay back the monthly subscription within the first three jobs of every month" [QuoteIQ HVAC 2026]. MasterBook can credibly claim **"one saved no-show pays a year of subscription"** — at 1 500 ₽ ticket × 1 saved/yr ≥ 1 990 ₽ annual.

A salon doing 25 weekly appointments at $65 with 15 % no-show loses **$31 187/yr** [AgentZap]. Even the solo end of that range dwarfs any indie CRM sticker.

## 2. Value-vs-price gap tactics from companies users overpay for

Distilled tactics that drive "I'd happily pay more" psychology:

1. **Frame against a hated incumbent.** Linear positions every screen against Jira's complexity; Superhuman against Gmail's noise. The competitor is the price anchor — not your own cheaper tier. Patrick Campbell (Profitwell): "willingness to pay" rises when users can articulate what they're *escaping* [Acquired podcast; Intercom]. For MasterBook: anchor against **Excel + WhatsApp + paper notebook** ("the chaos") not against other CRMs.

2. **Tier-name as identity, not feature count.** GlossGenius uses Standard/Gold/Platinum [Webflow.glossgenius]; Notion uses Plus/Business/Enterprise. Names signal "who you are now". Avoid generic "Lite/Standard" — use names a master wants to *be*: **Старт / Мастер / Студия / Premier**.

3. **Bundle so the must-have is one tier up.** GlossGenius locks intake forms and Google booking behind the $48 Gold tier vs $24 Standard — a $24 jump for one feature that nearly every operator needs [Webflow.glossgenius; Vagaro.com/learn]. Result: ~80 % of solos buy Gold, feeling Standard was a decoy. Lesson: place **online booking link** in PRO not Free.

4. **High anchor + obvious middle.** Lenny's Newsletter ran a $200 tier next to a $350 "Insider" tier and a $10 000 reference value — the $200 felt obvious [StartupSpells]. Three-tier display with a "decoy / hero / anchor" structure averages ~30 % revenue lift per customer [getmonetizely.com; Potio].

5. **Free-trial-without-card, but with a sunset.** Linear and Superhuman use a no-card trial yet end it with a soft paywall, not a hard wall. Customers leaving SaaS pricing untouched for years quietly lose 20–40 % WTP [Profitwell research; Mind the Product]. Trial → ask card on day 12, charge day 14, with **two-tap cancel** to remove the "trap" feeling.

6. **Show ROI in your UI, not just on the pricing page.** Superhuman shows "time saved" weekly. For MasterBook: dashboard widget "**В этом месяце ты заработал 87 400 ₽ и не забыл 12 клиентов**". This converts product into ongoing proof-of-value — the killer of churn.

7. **Public price, no "Contact sales".** Indie buyers distrust hidden pricing. Linear lists every tier publicly; Jira's complex pricing is a chronic source of resentment [tierly.app teardown].

## 3. Outcome pricing — three options, weighed

| Model | Pros | Cons | Verdict for MasterBook |
|-------|------|------|-----------------------|
| **Pay only on weeks with ≥3 confirmed bookings** | Highest alignment — vendor incentive = customer outcome [L.E.K.; Aquant] | Revenue volatility; off-season collapse; complex billing infra [Bain; Lago] | **No** as primary — try as a "Comeback" reactivation tier |
| **"Save 10h/mo or refund"** | Risk reversal lifts conversions; 43 % of enterprise buyers value risk-share [getmonetizely 2026 guide] | Hard to measure on mobile; fraud risk | **As a marketing guarantee on annual plans only** |
| **1 % of incoming online deposits, no monthly fee** | Zero friction to start; scales with success; matches Stripe/Square mental model | Requires payments integration (Russian payment rails: СБП, ЮKassa); cannibalizes flat-rate ARPU | **Optional add-on at v1.4+**, not core model |

Industry consensus: **hybrid is the right destination — flat base + outcome-linked variable**. Chargebee projects 61 % of SaaS will be hybrid by end-2026 vs only 9 % fully outcome-based [getmonetizely]. So: keep 299 ₽/mo flat, layer optional СБП-deposit fee (e.g. 0.5 %) once payments ship in v1.4.

## 4. Anchoring price up — what goes in ELITE 1 490 ₽

The job of the ELITE tier is **to never be bought** — only to make PRO 299 ₽ feel laughably cheap. The anchor effect lifts ~30 % ARPU when the top tier sits ~5× above the hero tier [getmonetizely; Potio]. 1 490 ₽ is exactly 5× 299 ₽ — psychologically optimal.

ELITE should contain things masters **fantasize about**, not just heavy-power features:

- **Custom booking domain** (`anna.masterbook.ru` → `anna.online`) — status symbol
- **Priority WhatsApp support** with named human, < 4 h SLA
- **Branded SMS sender ID** (replaces "MasterBook" with "АННА")
- **White-label client-facing booking page** (no MasterBook footer)
- **Multi-staff** up to 5 (covers the "I'm hiring an apprentice" dream)
- **AI assistant**: auto-drafts re-engagement messages, suggests prices, summarises week
- **Business analytics**: cohort retention, top-services revenue breakdown
- **Tax PDF auto-export** monthly to FNS-compatible format
- **Concierge migration** from Excel/WhatsApp on signup

Critically, only **3 of these** need to actually exist at launch — the rest can be "Coming Q3" in the tier card. The anchor works on perception, not delivery.

## 5. Counter-anti-patterns that murder pricing power

From Profitwell/Flexprice/SeatCompress writeups:

- **Free tier too generous.** "Free up to 30 clients" is borderline — solo masters often have exactly 30–60 active clients. Most won't upgrade. Tighten to **20 clients OR 30-day-old data deletion on Free**. Flexprice: "capping usage or storage and holding back key features is vital" [Flexprice].
- **Hidden upcharges.** Vagaro's à-la-carte gets criticised for $X here + $Y there [Vagaro.com vs GlossGenius]. Don't sell add-ons during checkout. Bundle inside tiers.
- **Surprise auto-renewal.** 60 % of SaaS contracts renew at inflated counts because no notice [SeatCompress]. Send email 7 + 3 + 1 days before charge with cancel link. Russian audiences are *especially* prickly on this — app-store reviews are weaponised.
- **Downgrade friction.** Let users downgrade in-app with **one tap, no "are you really really sure"** dialogs. Proactive usage alerts at 70 %/85 %/100 % of tier prevent the "you got blocked at 6 am Sunday" 1-star.
- **Pricing left unchanged for years.** Patrick Campbell: companies that don't revisit price every 6–12 months silently lose 20–40 % of revenue [Profitwell; saasclub.io]. Plan a price review at 6 mo post-launch.
- **Per-seat for a solo audience.** Don't price per-user when 95 % of users are 1-person. Flat tier with optional "+ extra seat" add-on.

## 6. Russian pricing display: does "5 ₽/день" backfire?

The cost-of-coffee framing **works for entertainment subscriptions** and **backfires for tools**. Yandex Plus runs at 449 ₽/mo (≈ 15 ₽/day) and is overtly marketed as a lifestyle perk bundle [yandex.ru/support/plus-ru; plus.yandex.ru/action/plus_update_2026]. Tinkoff Pro at 299 ₽/mo (10 ₽/day) is positioned as "цена меньше чашки кофе" [tbank.ru/pro; vsezaimyonline.ru]. Both work because the buyer is a **consumer**, not a business operator.

For MasterBook, the audience is *earning money with the tool*. The mental frame should be:

> «299 ₽ в месяц — это один пропущенный клиент в год»

This is ROI framing — far stronger than coffee framing for a professional. RusBase and vc.ru reviews of Russian SaaS [vc.ru/topstud, vc.ru/services/2331353] show that for self-employed, the **"платит за себя с одного клиента"** angle dominates positive reviews. Reserve coffee framing for the **annual upgrade nudge** ("1 990 ₽/год = 5 ₽/день") because that's where loss aversion bites, not the primary headline.

Russian context for sanity-check: ПрофиГид 270 ₽/mo, OkoCRM от 380 ₽, Master CRM 360 ₽, Evolutrix 189 ₽ [vc.ru/topstud/2726353]. 299 ₽ sits in the cluster — to **feel** under-priced you need 2–3× the perceived value-per-ruble at the same price, then anchor up.

## 7. Concrete recommendation — 4-tier ladder for MasterBook

| Tier | Price | Audience | Hard limits | Headline feature gates |
|------|-------|----------|-------------|------------------------|
| **СТАРТ** (Free) | 0 ₽ | "Try-before-believe" | 20 clients · 50 appts · 1 service category · local-only data | Clients, appointments, manual reminders, JSON export |
| **МАСТЕР** (Pro) | **299 ₽/mo · 1 990 ₽/yr** | The 80 % hero tier | Unlimited clients · Supabase cloud sync · 1 device pair | Auto-reminders, photo gallery, finance + charts, biometric lock, profession packs, tax PDF |
| **СТУДИЯ** (Business) | **690 ₽/mo · 5 990 ₽/yr** | Multi-room solos, growth-stage | Up to 3 staff slots · branded booking link · CSV export | Online booking page, deposit collection (СБП), waitlist, no-show fee, smart reactivation, custom forms |
| **PREMIER** (Elite-anchor) | **1 490 ₽/mo · 12 900 ₽/yr** | The "showroom" anchor | Up to 5 staff · white-label · custom domain | AI assistant, branded SMS, priority WhatsApp support, advanced analytics, concierge migration, monthly tax filing automation |

**Gating logic, justified:**

- **Free caps at 20 clients, not 30** — Russian competitor benchmark is 50–unlimited; under-cutting on quantity is wrong, we differentiate on polish. 20 forces upgrade *before* the user feels invested enough to switch to a competitor.
- **Cloud sync is the killer Free→Pro gate** because it removes the most painful single fear (losing data) — exactly the highest-WTP job in §1.
- **Online booking link sits in Studio not Pro** — same play as GlossGenius putting forms in Gold. This is the 690 ₽-tier conversion engine.
- **Multi-staff in Studio (3) → Premier (5)** monetises the "I'm growing" fantasy without alienating true solos.
- **AI assistant only in Premier** — keeps marginal-cost AI bills out of the cheap tier and gives the anchor a sci-fi halo.
- **Yearly discount = ~5 months free** (1 990 vs 3 588) — aggressive enough to cement retention, justifies the "5 ₽/день" annual line in copy.

To make MasterBook universal across professions without losing the beauty roots, **profession-pack the gates, not the tiers**: each profession (tutor, photographer, plumber, vet, etc.) gets a free "pack" of templates (service list defaults, contract snippets, intake form) installable at onboarding. The tiers themselves are profession-agnostic — only the seed content changes. This is how Notion templates work, and why Notion feels universal despite being used wildly differently per audience.

**Net effect:** MasterBook's hero tier sits at the bottom of the Russian competitor cluster (299 ₽), delivers ~25 700 ₽/mo of stacked job-value, anchors against a 1 490 ₽ aspirational tier, and converts on cloud-sync fear and online-booking ambition rather than on lock-in. That is what makes a $4 product feel like a $12 product — and lets you raise to 499 ₽ in year 2 without losing the base.
